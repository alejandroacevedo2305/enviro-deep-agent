# 4. [8] List of carcinogenic and suspected
### carcinogenic compounds

This appendix contains an overview of compounds
which on the basis of carcinogenic, mutagenic and/or
teratogenic properties have been classified with respect
to carcinogenicity by the European Community ()
or by the International Agency for Research on Cancer
().

Within the  classification system, the coding means:
 carcinogenic to humans on the grounds of
epidemiological research findings;
 carcinogenic to humans as based on evidence in
particular from chronic animal testing;
 suspected of being carcinogenic to humans;
 no or too little evidence available for classification
into one of the above-mentioned classes.

The  coding comprises compounds which are:
 carcinogenic to humans;
 ‘probably’ carcinogenic to humans;
 ‘possibly’ carcinogenic to humans;
 cannot be classified with respect to being
carcinogenic to humans;
 probably not carcinogenic to humans.

The substances that are included in the table without

an  or  classification are under consideration.
The coding between parentheses refers to the relevant
 file number, where:
xxx information available on carcinogenicity of
the substance

xxx modification of the classification is under
consideration

xxx solvent

xxx pesticide

**Sources**

The information is based on: ‘H. Roelfzema, Min. van

WVC, Directoraat-Generaal van de Volksgezondheid,

Directie Voeding en Veiligheid van produkten, June 1991’.

The compounds given in _italics_ in Appendix 8 are

carcinogenic according to Appendix A of the Compulsory

Registration of Hazardous Substances, module A5 of the

Occupational Health and Safety Information System for
