**1. Evaluación cumplimiento normativo, fuentes móviles.**

Para realizar una evaluación del cumplimiento normativo correspondiente al flujo de las
fuentes móviles a través de los caminos interiores del predio (Parque Industrial) que
conecta las rutas de entrada y salida de camiones, se utilizará como norma de referencia
la **Normativa de la Confederación Suiza OPB 814.41**, ya que al analizar fuentes móviles,
no procede su evaluación a través del D.S. No 38/2011 del MMA, según lo establece en su
Art. No 5, letra a:

“Artículo 5°.- La presente norma no será aplicable al ruido generado por:

a) La circulación a través de las redes de infraestructura de transporte, como, por ejemplo,
el tránsito vehicular, ferroviario y marítimo”.

La Normativa de la Confederación Suiza, **OPB 814.41**, en su Art. No 43, plantea un valor
límite de inmisión sonora para zonas con diferentes graduaciones de sensibilidad, los

cuales se definen a continuación:

 - Grado de sensibilidad I : Zonas que requieren una mayor protección
contra el ruido, especialmente, en las zonas de descanso.

 - Grado de sensibilidad II : Zonas donde ninguna empresa perjudicial es
autorizada, especialmente en las zonas de habitación, así como en aquellas
reservadas a las construcciones e instalaciones públicas.

 - Grado de sensibilidad III : Zonas donde se admiten empresas
medianamente perjudiciales, especialmente en las zonas de habitación y
artesanales (zonas mixtas) así como en las zonas agrícolas.

 - Grado de sensibilidad IV : Zonas donde se admiten empresas
extremadamente perjudiciales, especialmente en las zonas industriales.

**Road (ISO-road 9613)**

**Definition**

A road is an item used for modelling the sound pressure level emission of roads. The
sound emission can be given in sound power per metre, but also in number of vehicles per
hour, speed and surface type. If the number of vehicles is used, the sound emission is
automatically calculated according to the RMV-SRM2 standard.

The RMV-SRM2 standard defines 4 categories of vehicles. These categories are motor
cycles, light vehicles, light trucks and heavy trucks.

- Light vehicle - cars, vans with a single rear axle with a maximum of 2 wheels on it;

- Light truck - buses, coaches, non-articulated lorries with a single rear axle;

- Heavy truck - articulated vehicles, vehicles with more than one rear axle excluding buses

and coaches.

**Properties**

- The name and description of the road are used for selection and controlling purposes. It is
good practice to keep the name unique.

- The co-ordinates of the road are given by as many points as needed to define the
polynomial line. The co-ordinates are viewed and modified in the co-ordinate list.

- height definition choices are: 'Absolute height', 'Relative height', 'Relative to item below'
and 'User defined', see also terrain model.

- The height of a road requires two properties:

 - the terrain level of all road points compared to the fixed 0-level. This value has to be
equal or more than zero. All points can have a different terrain level. Entering the ISO
height (only possible with height definition 'User defined') all points will have the same
terrain level;

 - the height of the road points above this terrain level. All points can have a different
height. Entering the ISO height all points will have the same height.

**Distribution** :

The speed per category, the road surface, the gradient correction
and the total 24 hour traffic flow have to be entered. In addition,
the average hourly distribution, as a percentage of the total 24 hour
traffic flow, has to be entered for each period. Finally, the average
hourly distribution per category as a percentage of the flow in that
period has to be entered. The sound power per period in dB(A)/m is
then calculated automatically according to the RMV/SRM II method.

**Traffic flow** :

The speed per category, the road surface type, the gradient
correction and the average hourly traffic flow per period have to be
entered. The sound power per period in dB(A)/m is then calculated
automatically according to the RMV/SRM II method.

**Sound power** : The sound power per period in dB(A)/m can be entered directly.
The road surface type of a road can be selected out of a list based on the RMV/SRM II
method. To modify existing surface types, or to add new surface types, click on the button
next to the selection list. After clicking on the button the road surface corrections form

will be shown.

The gradient correction provides the adjustment for the extra noise from traffic on a
gradient expressed in dB(A). This correction should only be applied for upward flow. The
gradient correction should be calculated with the formula: Cg = 5log(G)-1.5, where G is the
percentage of the gradient. So a gradient of 10 percent gives a correction of 3.5 dB(A).

**The prediction**

The attenuation between the sources and the receivers will be calculated. The total

calculated attenuation is subtracted from the emission to determine the expected sound
pressure level at the receiver points. For roads first a pre-run is performed to convert the
road into several point sources. This is done by creating 1 point source per segment. At
the start location and the end location of a road there is always a point source created.
The segment angle used for the point sources in between start and end is given in the
calculation settings.

**Calculation**

The sound power per category of vehicles is calculated as follows:

Lw = 10 * lg (Q/V) + a + b*V + Cwh
Cwh = Max (Cw, Ch) if Ch <> 0, Cw if Ch= 0
Ch = 5 * lg(ph) − 1.5

Q Average traffic flow in number of vehicles per hour
V Average speed in km/hour
a Non-speed-dependent part of the sound power for 1 vehicle in dB(A)
b Speed-dependent part of the sound power for 1 vehicle in dB(A)*hour/km
Cwh Combined correction for surface type and slope in dB

Cw Surface correction in dB

Ch Slope correction in dB
ph Slope in %

The attenuation between the road and the receivers will be calculated, whereby the road
is divided into point sources according to the angle of sight calculation parameter. The
total calculated attenuation, combined for all point sources of the road, is subtracted from
the total emission of the road, to determine the expected sound pressure level at the
receiver points.

**Road surface corrections (ISO 9613)**

**Definition**

Road surface corrections are corrections on the noise emission of the road as a result of

the chosen road surface type. Next to 12 standard road surface types the user can also
define own surface types with corresponding corrections.

**Use**

To edit existing surface types, or to add new surface types, click on the button next to the
road surface selection list on the road dialog form.

**Properties**

- The name and description of the road surface are used for selection purposes.

- The corrections are corrections per category and per octave on the noise emission.

- The speed dependency is used to distinguish between non, linear and logarithmic speeddependency.

**Ray-tracing Model**

The propagation terms (ΔLGU, ΔLOD, ΔLSW, ΔHousing and ΔLR) are calculated by means
of a ray-tracing model. This method is described briefly below:

1. Starting from the receiver, a number of imaginary lines (sound rays) are drawn. The
angle between two successive lines is called the sector's angle of sight, as defined by
RMV'96. The magnitude of this angle is defined by the parameter φ (phi), which varies
between 1° and 5°. The angle of sight has a default value of 2°. This preference-value may
be changed. In every sector an imaginary line is drawn from the receiver to (a segment of)

a line source.

2.Subsequently, the items (buildings, ground regions, etc.) intersected by the imaginary
line, are determined. These items influence the noise propagation (reflections, diffraction
and ground effects) and will be taken into account for the calculation.

3.Based on the intersected items, the calculations are carried out in order to determine
the propagation terms.

4.Contributions by reflecting objects are taken into account by the introduction of receiver
and source images, using all reflecting objects as a mirror. Each reflected propagation path
is again represented by an imaginary line, and dealt with according to steps 2 and 3 above.
The number of reflections to be accounted for may vary between 0 and 3, and has a
default value of 1. This preference-value can be changed.

**Noise Emission Level LE**

This is the noise emission by source height and by octave (RMV'96, §3.4). Noise emissions
depend on a number of factors, e.g., the type and number of trains and the type of railway
track support.

**Screening ΔLSW**

For cases in which the sound propagation is (partly) obstructed, the effect of barriers is
calculated according to RMV'96, §5.6. It is mainly determined by the path length
difference ε, taking downward curving of the propagation path into account. In cases of
double or multiple diffraction within a sector, only the most effective barrier is taken into

account.

The most effective barrier is determined as follows:

1.For each barrier, compare the path length difference (re the direct path) with the
minimum value for the path length difference εmin = -0.8487m1. Barriers with a path
length difference ε < -0.8487m will be ignored.

2.For each barrier with significant path length difference, the separate values for the
barrier attenuation at 250, 500 and 1000 Hz are added algebraically. The barrier having

the maximum sum will be considered as the most effective within the sector.

3.If this comparison is not decisive, all separate values for the barrier attenuation from 63
Hz to 8000 Hz will be added algebraically. The barrier having the maximum sum will be

considered as the most effective within the sector.

**Reflections ΔLR**

Contributions by reflecting objects are taken into account by the introduction of receiver
and source images, using reflecting buildings and barriers as a mirror. For each reflection,
attenuation due to absorption and dispersion is allowed for (RMV'96, §5.8). This
attenuation is determined as a function of the reflection factor of the reflecting object. If
the reflection factor equals 1, no attenuation is taken into account. If the reflection factor
equals 0, no contribution by reflection will be calculated.

It is possible to enter different values for the reflection factor in each separate octave. For
buildings, the preference-value for the reflection factor is 0.8 for each octave (the
attenuation per reflection is 1 dB).
